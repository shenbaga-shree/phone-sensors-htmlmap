What is auto-brightness?

 Auto-brightness is a feature on electronic devices, such as smartphones, tablets, and laptops, that
automatically adjusts the brightness of the screen based on the surrounding lighting conditions.

How does auto-brightness work?

 Auto-brightness uses ambient light sensors to detect the lighting conditions around the device and 
adjusts the screen brightness accordingly. When the device is in a bright environment, the screen
brightness will increase to make it easier to see. Conversely, when the device is in a dimly lit 
environment, the screen brightness will decrease to reduce eye strain and save battery life.

How does the system work?

 1.Light Detection: The ambient light sensor (usually located near the front-facing camera or screen
bezel) measures the intensity of surrounding light. This is typically quantified as a lux value.
 2.Signal Conversion: The sensor data is converted into a digital signal that the device's 
operating system can interpret.
 3.Algorithm Processing:The device’s processor interprets this electrical signal using built-in 
algorithms.
 4.Display Adjustment: The system calculates the optimal display brightness and seamlessly 
adjusts the current or backlight of the screen to match.
