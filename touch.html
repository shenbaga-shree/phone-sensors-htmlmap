How your phone detects touch?

Your phone uses a capacitive touchscreen that relies on your body's natural electricity, not 
physical pressure. Beneath the glass lies an invisible, microscopic grid of conductive wires 
(usually Indium Tin Oxide) that maintains a constant electrostatic field

 The Detection Process

When your finger touches the screen, it acts as a conductor. It absorbs a tiny amount of that 
electrical charge, creating a micro voltage drop at the exact point of contact. 
Sensors along the edges of the screen instantly detect this change and calculate the 
exact X and Y coordinates of your touch. This coordinate data is then sent to the phone’s processor, 
which translates it into an action—like opening an app, typing, or scrolling.
